Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is a species in the family Felidae; it is a muscular, deep-chested cat with a short, rounded head, a reduced neck and round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic.

White Rhinoceros

The white rhinoceros or square-lipped rhinoceros (Ceratotherium simum) is the largest extant species of rhinoceros. It has a wide mouth used for grazing. The northern subspecies has 50 remaining in the world.

Owl

Owls are birds from the order Strigiformes, which includes over 200 species of mostly solitary and nocturnal birds of prey typified by an upright stance, broad head, feathers adapted for silent flight and many others.

Clownfish

Clownfish are fishes from the subfamily Amphiprioninae in the family Pomacentridae. They all form symbiotic mutualisms with sea anemones. Thirty species are recognized in the genus Premnas, and genus Amphiprion.
